About My Website

This website documents my efforts of further understanding, and demonstrating, my ever-growing knowledge of topics related to artificial intelligence, machine learning, computer vision, and (sometimes) cybersecurity.
By no means am I an expert in any of these topics, but I hope to share my adventure of becoming one. Computer science is quite an interesting and wide-ranging field, and I hope my articles will be able to teach you a thing or two about these captivating topics.

About Me

tldr; I've been around computers my whole life and, as a result, developed a passion for optimizing everything in sight. My other passions - competitive gaming, motorsports, bodybuilding - fuel my min-maxing lifestyle. I graduated with a Cybersecurity-focused Computer Science degree, but I am pivoting to AI/ML because it brings me more joy and has far more positive impacts to our world.

My name is Sason (Sauce-on for the Iranians, Suh-sawn for everyone else) or Reza, whichever is easier. I'm a network automation engineer with a fascination in artificial intelligence, machine learning, cybersecurity, and motorsports. I graduated in 2018 with a cybersecurity-focused bachelor's in Computer Science and have since been working in the industry as a systems software engineer while refining my cybersecurity skills and learning new computer science subjects.

I have been surrounded by computers and technology my whole life, and, as a result, developed a genuine passion for learning, improving, and optimizing (also known as min-maxing). This passion stems from playing competitive video games, both online and against my brother/cousins, throughout my childhood - specifically Warcraft III, Overwatch, Rocket League, and even Toontown Online - which requires executing actions in the most optimal sequences. I was the youngest kid around so I was never allowed to play first. Instead, I watched attentively, analyzed their moves, and noted any mistakes or moves which could be optimized. When it was finally my turn to play, I would capitalize on their mistakes and optimize my moves to quickly beat them. My competitive gaming family turned me into the min-maxer I am today, and now I'm able to apply these analytical techniques to all aspects of my life and career.

Other than min-maxing, I enjoy tinkering and stress testing (commonly known as breaking things and then fixing them). Playing video games made me competitive and analytical but also sprung a small curiosity in me regarding my limits: “To what extent are my skills limited? Well, I don't know, let's play for hours on end until I find out.” After countless hours playing a video game, I learned what I was capable of and now it was time to see what the game was capable of. I started thinking of how I could go beyond the limits of the video game and do things that I wasn't necessarily supposed to do. While most of it was silly stuff, such as feeding my Chao's infinite chaos drives in Sonic Adventure 2, it taught me to think outside the box. And it's been the same story since, but now I test the limits of everything in sight, rather than only video game functionality.

Cyber security and artificial intelligence are exactly the fields to fuel my competitive, curious mindset: Never-ending amounts of applications to tinker with and break, unique and creative methodologies to learn from created by people all over the world, and the competition to get your name out there. All of the other Computer Science fields simply can't match the excitement of cyber security and artificial intelligence.
